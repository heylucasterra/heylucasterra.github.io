Building and leading Nubank's credit card design team

Joining credit card

When I joined Nubank, my interest for human behaviour, business, data models and the dry access to credit in my home country swiftly drew me to focus on Nubank’s core product: the credit card.

Back then, designers were focused on shipping new products and I was excited to push our core product to the next level.

Embracing leadership

As the company jumped into hypergrowth, I organically leaned towards leadership and was fortunate to help build this team from the ground up.

Credit card has been my home at Nubank for the past 3 years and the best team of product designers, user researchers and content strategists I have ever worked with.

1. Building a strong design team

Finding the right people

In 2018, Nubank hit puberty and was starting to grow fast. As with any adolescent, different parts of the body grew faster than others. Design was one that took longer to develop.

The first three people I helped hire (in the picture above) were two product designers and a UX researcher. We all had a very complementary set of personalities, backgrounds and technical skills, which was super helpful for asking for help and getting a different perspective on the problems we had at hand.

We matched our different skills and personal interests with specific moments of people’s journey with their credit cards. For example, Diego’s deep apreciation for details made him the best person to lead design for our rewards program while my interest in data and business led me to stay closer to how our limit increase policies should work.

A few months in and it was now their time to also help find the best talent our there, so I shared my thoughts and learnings from interviewing design candidates.

Evolving our rituals

Fastforward to when the team had doubled in size (around 6 people) and our rituals had evolved a ton. We also started sharing some of the housekeeping tasks so everybody was a leader in one direction. Diego and Ana, for example took over our team retrospectives and Amanda was nominated the queen of design critiques, helping team members ensure they were making the most out of their design crits.

Working as a remote team

More recently, during the pandemic we all struggled with calendar tetris, making space for collaboration and getting to know the new people joining the team. Ana, Rafa and Poly then organized an online “Design Studio Day” block on Thursdays so that we always have time in the week to focus in our work, gather feedback, learn more about what others are working on and get to know one another.

It takes time to make a team work, and even more time to make it work out. The integration an collaboration that comes with spending Thursdays together as a team has really paid off on the long-run.
